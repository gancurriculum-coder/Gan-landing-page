What We're Building

The Jewish homeschool curriculum families have been asking for.

Gan HaChochma is a complete, print-first homeschool curriculum that brings together serious Judaic studies and strong secular academics — designed by a curriculum and instruction professional with deep roots in both worlds. No denominational agenda. No Christian-coded framing. Just honest, thoughtful, beautiful Jewish education for your home.

```
📜

Torah & Tanakh

Parsha study, Tanakh text, Jewish values, and classical mefarshim — taught with warmth and intellectual seriousness across all grade levels.

🕍

Jewish History & Israel

From Avraham to the modern State — age-appropriate, spiral-designed, and honest. Including antisemitism, Zionism, and how to talk about all of it.

🔤

Hebrew Language

Modern Ivrit as a living language alongside tefillah Hebrew — starting from early childhood, building toward fluency with pride.

🔬

Secular Academics

Real science, ELA, history, and more — taught through a Jewish lens where it fits naturally, with clean secular worksheets for charter school documentation.

✡️

Holidays & Halacha

The Jewish calendar as a living curriculum spine. Shabbat, Yom Tov, and mitzvot presented as normative, beautiful, and alive — not as religion class.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Multi-Age Friendly

Designed so families with children across grade bands can learn together — with differentiated tracks for Early Childhood through High School.

```

Who It's For

Built for the full breadth of the traditional Jewish home.

  • Families across the traditional spectrum — Modern Orthodox, Conservative, Mizrachi, Sephardic, and affiliated independents
  • Homeschoolers who want Jewish life woven through their whole curriculum, not siloed into "religious school"
  • Families using public charter schools who need clean, documentable secular materials alongside their Judaic studies
  • Parents with limited Judaics background who want support teaching what they weren't taught themselves
  • Families tired of building everything from scratch — or patching together materials that weren't made for them
  • Communities and co-ops looking for a shared curriculum with a consistent Jewish voice
